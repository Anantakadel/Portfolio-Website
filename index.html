About Me

Hello, I'm Ananta Kadel — a dedicated developer and technology enthusiast committed to delivering innovative solutions and continuous professional growth.

I’m a BIT final-year student with a strong interest in networks, connectivity, systems, and the people who depend on them. I’m currently gaining hands-on experience as a Technical Support Intern at Vianet, where I work with real-world connectivity issues, user-side troubleshooting, and system-level problems.

I might not always be able to connect in love relationships—but when it comes to internet connections, networks, and troubleshooting, that’s my zone. I enjoy understanding how systems communicate, why networks fail, and how to restore stable, secure connectivity.

Alongside my internship, I’m actively learning computer networking fundamentals, building skills in network troubleshooting, routing and switching concepts, Linux systems, and IT infrastructure, with the goal of growing into a Networking Engineer.

I believe good networks are invisible when they work—and unforgettable when they don’t. My focus is to make sure they work.
